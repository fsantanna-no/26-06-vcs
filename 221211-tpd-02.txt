Reviewer: 4

Recommendation: Reject

Comments:
This paper proposes a permission-less consensus mechanism for a decentralized content authoring system. The unique angle brought by this paper is the use of content production or human effort as a scarce resource in designing a bitcoin like consensus mechanism. However, the proposed mechanism's byzantine fault tolerance capabilities are not theoretically proved.

Furthermore, there are practical limitations of using content production as a scarce resource in the protocol. At the core of the consensus model proposed in this paper is the assumption that producing content requires non-trivial amount of human effort, hence, it is a scarce resource. However, a number of practical factors make this assumption weaker, hence, weaking the sybil resiliency of the protocol. First, the proliferation of generative neural models for text and image is lowering the barrier for producing seemingly legitimate content, hence, lowering the barrier for bots (or human operated "like farms") to obtain "reputation" (the currency in the proposed platform). Second, the observed power-law nature of content producer/consumer in various online platforms suggest it will be possible for a small fraction of users to obtain a large portion of total reputation (the currency proposed in this paper) available in the system. A direct consequence of a skewed distribution of reputation is the ease of recruiting sybils for small part of the population. From the paper, it is unclear if the system will still remain sybil attack resilient at the presence of skewed distribution of reputation. If a skewed distribution of reputation makes it possible to easily launch sybil attacks then that will incentivize possible attackers.

Given that the proposed consensus mechanism is not mathematically proved to be byzantine fault tolerant and can possibly breakdown or incentive sybil attack under practical scenarios, significant work may be required to improve the paper. Hence, the decision to reject.

Reviewer: 5

Recommendation: Author Should Prepare A Major Revision For A Second Review

Comments:
This paper proposes a reputation-based sybil attack defense mechanism for P2P content-sharing platforms. It adopts blockchain's longest-chain idea to order user posts and defines a reputation scheme to mitigate malicious users.  

The paper is good overall but I have the following concerns that should be addressed, if possible:
1- There are many similar work in the literature that propose to use of blockchain for online social networks with reputation systems such as SteemIt, Sapien, and LIT. This paper should clearly identify the unique differences. Also, please extend related work to cite more related work in this area.
2-  Scalability: I don't see any analysis in terms of scalability. Is the proposed system scalable to 100s of users? Also, it seems like it's possible for all reps in the system will be spent via dislike. Are there any mechanisms in the proposed solution to overcome such situations?
